Many people haven't heard of it yet: **cocktail catering** – a professional mobile bar that comes to you. Not just a bartender with a shaker, but a complete bar setup that can be set up anywhere. For corporate parties, summer festivals, anniversaries or client events. If you're hearing about it for the first time, you might wonder: what's actually possible? Here's an overview.

## 1. Live Bar with Bartender Show

A bar where something actually happens: flair bartending, shaker tricks, sometimes even a bit of fire. Guests stop, chat – the standing reception turns into a real event. Many providers set up the bar flexibly: classic with a wooden counter or modern with LED lighting. Signature drinks with company logo or brand colours are also possible.

## 2. Theme Night: "Around the World"

A theme that's easy to pull off: each station represents a country. Mexico with tacos and tequila, Japan with sake, Brazil with caipirinha, Italy with Aperol and antipasti. Guests wander from station to station – and automatically have something to talk about. Works especially well for international teams or companies with offices worldwide.

## 3. After-Work on the Roof

Roof access is often underestimated. A rooftop party in summer almost always works – the view alone sets the mood. A mobile bar can be set up on parking decks, flat roofs and terraces. The equipment comes with the provider, you provide the location. Sundowner drinks, alcohol-free options – all possible.

## 4. Blind Tasting as Team Building

Who can taste the lime? Who recognises basil? In blind tasting, small teams compete to guess ingredients or classic cocktails. An experienced bartender hosts, explains a bit about spirits and flavours along the way – optionally with an award ceremony at the end. Works well for team events or as a workshop block at larger conferences.

## 5. Winter Wonderland with Hot Drinks

Mulled wine always works – but there's more. Hot Buttered Rum, Apple Cinnamon Mule, alcohol-free punch variations. Mixed live, with matching décor, fire bowls or other winter extras on request. Works indoors and outdoors. In winter especially, guests appreciate something that warms them up – and that doesn't come from a bottle.

## 6. Lounge Instead of Stiff Programme

Business events don't have to be formal. Instead of a packed schedule: a cocktail lounge with comfortable seating, low-key music and a selection of good drinks. The bar adapts to the space – showroom, foyer or terrace. Good for client evenings, soft openings or internal networking.

## 7. DIY Cocktail Station

Guests mix their own – with instructions, ingredients and a bit of competition. Creativity and taste count; a small jury judges at the end. Equipment and recipe cards are provided, prizes or give-aways can be added. Works well as team building or as a break-out station at larger events.

## 8. Green Event – Sustainable, But Not Boring

Regional ingredients, reusable glasses, plastic-free garnishes. Sounds like compromise, but it doesn't have to be. Organic gin with herbs from the market, homemade syrups, creative alcohol-free alternatives – sustainable events can taste just as good. Many providers offer such concepts.

## 9. Pop-up Bar on the Company Lawn

Why rent expensive venues? A car park, courtyard or patch of grass often does the job. A pop-up bar comes with counter, lounge furniture, fairy lights – whatever you need. Venue costs disappear, the summer party feeling stays. The surprise of a real bar suddenly standing "right outside our door" usually goes down well.

## 10. Company Anniversary with a Custom Drink

10, 25 or 50 years – that deserves something special. A signature cocktail that matches the brand: ingredients, colours and name developed individually. Mixed live, optionally with recipe cards or personalised glasses. A drink like that sticks in people's minds – and in photos.

## In Short

Cocktail catering is more than just drinks: it's a mobile bar that comes to you, and a concept that fits your event. Whether it's a show, a theme night or simply a bar where people stop and start talking – the options are varied.
